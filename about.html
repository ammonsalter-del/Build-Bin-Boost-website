About the Simulation

Teaching R&D portfolio management through experiential learning

What is Build, Bin, Boost?

Build, Bin, Boost is an interactive business simulation where players take the role of Head of R&D at ShieldTech India, a home security technology company. Over two simulated years, players must build a portfolio of innovation projects, bin the ones that aren't working, and boost the winners—all while navigating committee politics, budget constraints, and strategic priorities.

The simulation runs entirely in a web browser as a single HTML file. No installation, no accounts, no cost. It is designed for business school teaching and is licensed under Creative Commons for non-commercial educational use.

The Scenario

Your predecessor at ShieldTech India was dismissed after years of poor results—15 projects funded, only 4 successful. The CEO has given you a budget and full authority to redesign how projects are selected and managed.

The board has set four strategic priorities:

Defend Metro Leadership

Retain position in top cities against competitors

Tier 2/3 City Rollout

Profitable entry into emerging cities

Recurring Revenue

Shift from hardware sales to services and subscriptions

AI-First Products

Lead the transition to intelligent security

Research Foundations

The simulation is informed by academic research on:

  • R&D project selection — How organisations evaluate and choose which innovation projects to fund
  • Decision-making in R&D — The cognitive and organisational factors that shape funding decisions
  • Panel dynamics — How selection committees function, including groupthink, advocacy, and consensus-building
  • Role tensions — Conflicts between different functional perspectives (technical, commercial, financial, strategic) in project evaluation
  • Active portfolio management — The importance of ongoing monitoring, resource reallocation, and project termination decisions
  • Strategic alignment — Connecting R&D investments to corporate strategy and balancing short-term delivery with long-term capability building
  • AI in project evaluation and selection — The potential benefits and costs of using AI as an assistant in R&D project selection processes

Why We Built This

Most business simulations are expensive, proprietary, and locked behind commercial licences. We believe high-quality educational tools should be accessible to everyone—students at under-resourced institutions, independent learners, and educators who want to experiment with their teaching.

This simulation was developed as part of ongoing research into innovation management education at Warwick Business School. It is released under a Creative Commons licence that allows free use, adaptation, and sharing for non-commercial purposes.
